20. Euseby ISHAM was born on 11 Dec 1486 in Ringstead, Northamptonshire, England. He died on 4 Sep 1546 in Pytchley, Northamptonshire, England. He was buried after 11 Dec 1546 in All Saints' Church, Pytchley, Northamptonshire, England. Euseby married Anne POULTON in 1515 in All Saints Church, Northamptonshire, England. [Parents]

EUSEBY ISHAM of Pytchley, gentleman, son and heir of Thomas Isham and Ellen Vere, lived at Ringstead nearly all his life upon a leasehold farm of the Mordaunts. His will of 16 Aug. 1546 was proved 11 Dec. 1546,11 apparently by Anne the relict and Robert Isham the son, whom he made his executors. In this will he asked for burial in the church of All Saints in Pytchley, and gave legacies to the poor folk of Isham, Pytchley and Grafton.

His wife, as appears by the brass of his son John Isham, was Anne Pulton, eldest daughter of Giles Pulton of Desborough, Northants, esquire, by Katherine, daughter of Thomas Lovett of Astwell, which Giles by his will of 12 Feb. 1548 made Robert Isham, the parson of Pytchley, one of his overseers. She survived her husband. Her son the parson of Pytchley gave a legacy of a mark to the parish of Desborough whereoute one of my parents did proceade.'

He is said on his son John's monument to have had twenty children, of whom we can reckon five sons and five daughters.

M iv Giles ISHAM was born in 1519 in Ringstead, Northamptonshire, England. He died on 31 Aug 1559 in Knotting, Bedfordshire, England. He was buried in 1559 in Pytchley, Northamptonshire, England.

GILES ISHAM of Pytchley, esquire, son and heir of Euseby Isham. His father brought him up in learning and sent him to London to study the law, whereby he so profited that on his father's death he retired to Pytchley, being in the commission of the peace and 'not a littell estemed of a great many and especially of Fraunces the then Earle of Bedford, whose steward he was.' He was member of parliament for Peterborough in the parliaments of 155 and 155%. He died 31 Aug. 1559 and was buried 1 Sept. at Pytchley.

He married Mary Watts of Knotting, co. Bedford, who was buried 11 June 1560 at Pytchley, by whom he had issue three daughters and coheirs.
M v Reverend Robert ISHAM was born before 1520 in Northamptonshire, England, United Kingdom. He died on 5 May 1564 in Pytchley, Northamptonshire, England. He was buried in Pytchley, Northamptonshire, England, United Kingdom.

Robert worked as a Chaplain to Queen Mary.

ROBERT ISHAM the parson of Pytchley was born at Ringstead and put to school at Rothwell, as appears by his will. He was a bachelor of arts in 1539 from Christ's College, Cambridge, and master of arts in 1542. He was instituted to the rectory of Grafton Underwood 6 July 1541 and to that of Islip 14 Dec. 1542. The living of Pytchley was held by him from 1548 until his death, and he was a chaplain and a prebendary of Peterborough from 1553 to 1559, when he resigned his stall. He was a chaplain to Queen Mary, who willingly axcepted him and was the cause of great credit and countenaunce unto him.'18 He died 5 May 1564, having made a will on 6 April 1564, which will was proved 3 Nov. 1568 by John and Henry Isham, his brothers, and Ferdinando Pulton, gentleman, a cousin. In this will he asked for burial in his church of All Saints, the will being a long and interesting one, with many legacies to friends and kinsfolk. To his brother Gregory's children he gave my two silver cups without covers doble gilt and eight silver spoones of one sorte wth round knobbes at thendes of them, and my square silver salte being doble gilte and my nest of bowles with one cover to them." To Jane Isham he gave my best silk curteyns which were her fathers and valence for a bed of nedlework and two quisshens of nedleworke.' To his aunt Calcot he gave his olde damaske cote furred,' and to his uncle Calcot ablak satten doublet.' 'The worser of my two gownes lyned w' blacke satten' was given to Sir Richard Ives, with the cloke that was Mr. Richards and all my bokes.' The parson of Barton was to have his tippet, the parson of Grafton his black velvet purse, the parson of Cranford his best satin nightcap, the parson of Woodford his best silk girdle, the parson of Orlingbury and the vicar of Dodington a Bible each and Sir Thomas Stincopp of Braunston a worsted furred cassock.' He names seven of his brothers and sisters as yet living, and gives a small legacy to nearly every soul in his parish of Pytchley. An inquest after his death was taken at Northampton 2 Oct. 1564," the three daughters of his elder brother Giles Isham being returned as his next heirs.
M vi Gregory ISHAM was born in 1520 in Ringstead, Northamptonshire, England, United Kingdom. He died on 4 Sep 1558 in Braunston, Northamptonshire, England, United Kingdom. He was buried on 6 Sep 1558 in Braunston, Northamptonshire, England.

Gregory worked as a Mercer and Merchant Adventurer in From 1537 to 1558 in London, England.

GREGORY ISHAM of Braunston, co. Northampton, gentleman, third son of Euseby Isham of Pytchley, by Anne Pulton, is named in his father's will of 1546, under which he had certain small legacies. He bought an estate at Braunston with lands in Farthingstone, Crick, Creaton and Old, and dying 4 Sept. 1558, aged thirty-eight years, was buried 6 Sept. at Braunston (M.I.). An inquest was taken after his death 20 Oct. 1558 at Kettering, whereby it 24 was found that he died seised of Rose manor in Braunston, of the manor of Braunston Newsted and of the advowson of Braunston, all of which he bought of Henry, Earl of Rutland. His will of 3 Sept. 1558 was proved 16 Nov. 1558" by his four brothers, the executors. He had been sometime a merchant in London, and reckons his merchandize in London and beyond sea as part of his personal estate, which he divides in three parts according to the custom of London, and as he left 20l. to the mercers' company for two dinners, he must have been a citizen free of their gild. The manor of Braunston with lands in Braunston, Farthingston, Old and Irchester were devised by him to his wife for life, his heirs succeeding to his lands in Creake and Creaton. An inventory of his goods is preserved at Lamport.

He married Elizabeth Dale, daughter of Matthew Dale of Bristol, and sister of William, John, Matthew and Henry Dale, to whom he gave legacies in 1558. She survived him and took as a second husband William Rosewell of Ford Abbey, solicitor-general to Queen Elizabeth, by whom she had issue. Gregory Isham had issue two sons and two daughters.
