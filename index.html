An Introduction to Robert

Robert's Isham ancestors had lived in Northamptonshire and London since at least 1300.

In 1621, a parish priest christened Robert in Cheshunt, a village 15 miles north of London. Robert's parents, William and Marie Isome, had moved to Cheshunt from London in the spring of 1620.

The death of his mother Marie in 1634 left Robert motherless at age 13. The following year, Robert voyaged from London to Virigina aboard a ship named the Globe. His shipmate on the voyage was his 20-year-old uncle Edward Cleborne, son of James Cleborne.

Robert labored as a servant in Virginia for several years. The work in Virginia was difficult. Marriage opportunities were few. With good reason, Robert returned to England. There, he settled in the village of Green's Norton, Northamptonshire.

Robert married Jane Clubber on 26 January 1643 in Green's Norton. Together, they had at least three children before Robert died at age 40 in April 1662. Six generations of descendants lived and labored in villages near Green's Norton.

Today, Robert has descendants throughout England, the United States, and Australia.

We are happy you dropped in! Enjoy learning about Robert, his ancestors, and his descendants.

Y-DNA Evidence Taught Us Robert's Story

In 2021, one of Robert's patrlineal descendants took a Y-DNA test that was critical in understanding the story of Robert's life. Before the DNA test, there was uncertainty about who Robert's parents and grandparents were, and we didn't know the story of his life before he married Jane Clubber in Green's Norton in 1642.

Growing Up in Cheshunt

Although researchers had known that a Robert Isome was christened in Cheshunt in 1621 as the son of William Isome and Marie Harding, they were reluctant to commit that this Robert was the same as the Robert who married Jane Clubber in 1642. The Y-DNA evidence confirmed that Robert of Green's Norton was the same Robert who was christened in Cheshunt. Further, it allowed us to identify William's parents.

Voyage to Virginia

Furthermore, the Y-DNA evidence showed that Robert of Cheshunt and Green's Norton was also the same Robert who sailed to Virginia on the Globe in 1635, accompanied by his uncle Edward Cleburne.

Robert and his Isham Ancestors

Many Ishams and Isoms lived in the Northamptonshire villages of Green's Norton, Wappenham, Greatworth, Blakesly, Chipping Warden, and Marston St. Lawrence. These are their Isham ancestors:

Robert Isham 1402-1475

Like his father before him, Robert was the escheator for the county of Northamptonshire. In his role as a justice of the peace for Northamptonshire, Robert often traveled to Westminster Palace in London where he met with King Henry VI, Richard Duke of York, and other important figures in the Wars of the Roses.

His first wife was Elizabeth de Knuston, by whom he had son William. By his second wife, Margaret, he had three other sons.

More

Wiliam Isham 1427-1510

William Isham of Pytchley, esquire, son and heir of Robert Isham, succeeded his father about 1475. William married Elizabeth widow of Thomas Bramspeth. Together, Wiliam and Elizabeth had at least three sons and one daughter.

More

Thomas Isham 1456-1512

His wife was Ellen Vere, daughter of Richard Vere of Addington and by Isabel, daughter of John Green of Drayton. She was probably married in 1485, for by a deed of 11 May 1485 the feoffees of William Isham's Northamptonshire lands gave them to his son Thomas Isham and Ellen his wife and the heirs of their bodies. They had two sons: 1) Euseby Isham of Pytchley, 2) John Isham, living in 1558 when his nephew Gregory gave him a legacy of five pounds.

More

Euseby Isham 1486-1546

Born in Pytchley, Northamptonshire, Euseby spent most of his life on a farm in the parish of Ringstead, Northamptonshire. He is said on his son John's monument to have had twenty children, of whom we know the names of five sons and five daughters.

More

Henry Isham 1528-1595

Like his older brothers Gregory and John, Henry was a succesful London mercer and merchant adventurer. Henry married Joan Bresley in London in 1557. Henry and Joan lived in the parish of St. Olave Old Jewry, where most of their children were christened. In 1587, he was appointed to the important role of Controller of the Port of London.

More

Apollonya Isham 1567 - 1595

Born in London in 1567, Apollonya moved to her father's estate on Dancer's Hill in South Mimms, probably around 1577. Around 1585 she had a relationship outside of marriage with James Cleburne, who was born in South Mimms. A son William was born of the union. In 1587 she married Robert Lee in South Mimms, and together they had a daughter Mary Lee. Mary Lee's marriage allegation in 1612 indicates that her guardian was Apollonya's sister Susan Isham of London.

More

William Isham/Isome 1586-1634

Born in South Mimms in about 1586 to Apollonya Isham and James Cleburne, William is believed to have been raised by his Aunt Susan Isham in London. Willam married Marie Harding in London in 1619 in the church of St. Peter, Paul's wharf, 1/2 mile from Susan Isham's parish of St. Olave, Old Jewry. Less than a year later, William and his wife moved to Cheshunt, just 1/2 mile from James Cleburne.
