Henry Green and Richard II

A sixth great-grandfather of Robert was Henry Green (1352-1399). He rose to prominence as a counsellor to King Richard II. When Henry of Bolingbroke (who would become King Henry IV) landed in England, he marched south and surrounded Bristol castle. He gave the occupants a night to leave the castle, by punishment of death. Henry Green did not surrender his post and was beheaded at the order of Henry of Bolingbroke on 30 July 1399.1

Henry Green has a substantial role in Shakespeare's historical play "King Richard II". You might enjoy watching the recent screen adaptation of King Richard II in the Hollow Crown series.
