Utah Sanctuaries

Mountain Dell

A branch of the Virgin Ward of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints was organized in the small settlement of Mountain Dell, Utah in 1863. Owen Isom was the branch president from 1869 to 1874, and Samuel Isom became the branch president in 1898.

Virgin

A church was built in Virgin, Utah in 1866. Samuel Isom served as bishop here from 1905 to 1907, when he became the bishop of the Hurricane ward.

Hurricane

A ward of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints was organized in Hurricane, Utah on 31 September 1907. The first bishop was Samuel Isom, who was transferred from the Virgin ward. In early 1908, the ward built a meeting hall ("The Hurricane Social Hall") on the southwest corner of State and Main Streets in Hurricane. Samuel Isom served as bishop until 1922.

St. George

The St. George temple was dedicated on 6 April 1877. On 18 May 1882, Owen and Elizabeth Howard Isom participated in ordinances in the temple. On 16 March 1883, a number of the living and deceased children of Owen and Elizabeth were sealed to them in the temple. On 24 March 1897, Katherine Wolfe Isom received her initiatory and endowment ordinances here. Richard Isom married Mary Eleanor Jepson in the St. George temple on 12 September 1905. On 13 June 1916 in the St. George temple, Richard and his siblings were sealed to their deceased parents, William Isom and Katherine Wolfe.
