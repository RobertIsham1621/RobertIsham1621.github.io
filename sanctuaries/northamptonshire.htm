Northamptonshire Churches

All Saints Church, Pytchley

Robert's Isham ancestors had lived in Pytchley as early as 1300. His ancestors who lived in Pytchley included Thomas Isham, William Isham, Robert Isham , Robert Isham, Robert Isham, and Henry De Isham.

Parts of the church date from the latter half of the 12th century.

Church of the Nativity of the Blessed Virgin Mary, Ringstead

Robert's great-grandfather Henry, son of Euseby Isham, was christened here.

Most of the building dates from the early part of the thirteenth century. The earliest parts of the church are the tower, the nave arcade, and the south door, all of which date to about 1240.

Church of St Bartholomew, Green's Norton

After returning from Virigina, Robert settled in Green's Norton. Robert married Jane Clubber here in 1643. It was here that his children were christened. It was here that Robert was buried when he died in 1662.

The Saxon church dates back to as early as the 600's and was in the royal domain named Norton Davey, used by Edward the Confessor and later, William the Conqueror. The entire village and church was sold to Robert's ancestor Sir Henry Green in 1335, renamed Norton Greene, and became the seat for the Greene family.

Church of St Mary, Wappenham

Robert's son William Isum was born in Green's Norton but moved to Wappenham and was buried in the churchyeard of the Church of St Mary

The church dates from the 14th century. It contains a monument to Robert's ancestor Thomas Billing, Chief Justice of England.

Church of St Peter, Greatworth

Robert's descendant William Isham moved from Wappenham to Greatworth, and his William's children were christened here.

Today the churchyard is peaceful meticulously maintained, and contains several gravestones for Ishams.
