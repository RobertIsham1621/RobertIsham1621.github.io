Northamptonshire Churches

All Saints Church, Pytchley

Robert's Isham ancestors had lived in Pytchley as early as 1300. In 1402, Owen Isom's 11th Great Grandfather, Robert Isham, was born in Pytchley, Northamptonshire. Robert was a justice of the peace for Northamptonshire. Robert Isham, justice of the peace for Northamptonshire, was the son of another Robert Isham, who was the escheator for Northamptonshire. Robert the escheator is described in the pleadings of his grandsons in 1486/7 as son of another Robert Isham and grandson of Henry Isham, and was probably the Robert Isham who was escheator (escheat: a common law doctrine which transfers the property of a person who dies without heirs to the crown or state) of Northamptonshire in 1391/2 and the Robert Isham to whom the King on 22 Nov. 1403 granted the custody of the manor of Cranford in this county. Robert purchased a dwelling and three virgates of land in Pytchley in 17 April 1412 from John Pulton and Joan, his wife.
